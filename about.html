SHERRIE FAY LIN



Growing up in the heart of the Silicon Valley, I’ve always admired those kids in class that were able to give powerpoint lectures their undivided attention, skip a level in math, ace their standardized tests, then get admitted early into their dream university.

I was never one of them; but, I felt pressured to run and win in the same race as them, sometimes (okay, a lot of the times). However, I stood unfazed in my dream to be a designer.

So, I paid my dues in the academic world, and soon enough I found myself seated as a graphic design and architecture major at the School of the Art Institute of Chicago.

It only took a semester for me to learn that it wasn’t the place for me to be at 18 years old.

It felt like the only thing I was learning was that there has to be more to design.

As cheesy as this sounds:

I thought college was the best and only way for me to become a designer; it wasn’t.

I thought I knew myself; I didn’t.

I couldn’t continue to burn thousands of dollars on an education that I no longer believed in, so I took a leap out of the box and embarked in redesigning my life.

For the next couple of years I embarked on throwing myself around the world in order to expand my worldview. I immersed myself in learning about life, love, culture, the analog society, and my identity.

Once I felt full with life, I took what I saw in the tangible world, combined it with my experiences, and was able to redefine my passion for design.

If there’s one power couple in the world, it’s design and technology.

I delved back into academia and sat in a room full of engineers and programmers to learn not only code and how to communicate with that side of the industry, but also to dive deeper as a visual designer.

With background in 2D/3D design, and now programming, I take a polymathic approach to design in technology.
