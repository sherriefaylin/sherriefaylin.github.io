DATABASE

We keep the user information stored in our database to a minimum (name, DOB, address), and it is collected when the user signs up.


Technology used: Mongo Database

DESIGN CONCEPT

Tbh, this app was born from a comical team discussion about making something that has truly never been made yet (at least to our knowledge).

I wanted to preserve the lighthearted intention in the overall design, so I decided to take elements from old retro ads you would have found glued on brick walls and combine them with the cleanliness of modern tech apps.

The main colors are muted hues of the classic compost and recycle bins sitting in your backyard.


Concept: Retro/modern

HOME PAGE

The home page is essentially a navigation dial. The user holds the center logo, and drags around the icons to pick the desired page. Once the user lets go on the selected icon, they will be redirected to the corresponding page in the app.

WALL & POST

The wall serves as a newsfeed. It consists of posts from users around them to a certain distance (this can be edited by tapping on the icon in the upper right corner).

To make a post, switch to the "post" tab and fill out the form. Once submitted, the post will append onto the wall.

MESSAGING

If a user taps on the "contact" button on a post, they will be promped with a an alert to continue. Then, a 1:1 message will be opened. Once the user on the other end confirms, this becomes an in-app chat room to contact each other.


Technology used: Socket.io

PROFILE

Users can access and edit their profile on the profile page.

This is also where the user can logout of the app.



Technology used: CRUD functionality

FUTURE DEVELOPMENT

Because this is a service app that relies heavily on the honor system, the process of developing an air-tight app was more like patching holes on a clear water balloon.

I delved into the ways of Airbnb, Uber, and Nextdoor in hopes of gaining perspective or even finding a solution to the inevitable privacy issues I could foresee if Rubbish were to be brought to life. Here is what I came up with:

THE ALARM SYSTEM

Context: A volunteer (User A) is heading over to another user's (User B) home to take their trash bins out. Both users' locations are allowed.

-Once A reaches within a 50 feet radius of B's property, B will receive a notification.

-In 5 minute increments, B will continue to receive notifications until A exits the property.
