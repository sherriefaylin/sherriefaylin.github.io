UNTRENDING

Re-filtering the news to encourage users to consume stories from multiple perspectives.

JANUARY 2019

FULL STACK NEWS FILTER APP

ROLE: Project Manager, Front-end Developer, UI/UX Designer



IDEA

It’s 2019- considering the impact of social media and the fact that politics has flooded into affecting our lives more directly than ever, it’s nearly impossible to stay politically ignorant.

As a team, we started our process by reflecting on the root of our personal political perspectives, and how we developed them over time. It wasn’t a surprise when we all found ourselves running in circles in our respective echo chambers.

While this isn’t necessarily a problem, it’s a type of ignorance. Unless you happen to be particularly interested in politics, most everyone naturally defaults to a perspective that may never change.

Additionally, social media is built to feed you what they know you want to see, which makes sense, of course. However, politically, it solidifies our opinions and what we believe is the “right” perspective as we continue to hone our politically awareness.

Untrending is designed to crack open that door of your echo chamber, or even just show you that there is another door and see a story from the other side.



DESIGN CONCEPT

As I was going through my first rounds of design research by studying the layouts and interfaces of my go-to news outlets, it didn’t take long for me to recognize that it was a misguided approach to designing Untrending. I realized that Untrending isn’t a newspaper- it’s more like an Instagram filter for your newspaper. So, for this app, I felt the importance of stripping it down to spotlight its main purpose: the perspective function.

So, I kept the elements on the web page to a minimum, and designed Untrending as a dynamic single page application.



TECHNOLOGY

This app is coded with HTML, Javascript, Express, mySQL2, Sequelize, Passport Google Authorization, and pulls from the News and REST Countries APIs.


ALGORITHM

When a user creates their account, they will select their position on the political spectrum. Their position will be translated into a number from 1-10 (1 = liberal, 10 = conservative) which will become the origin variable to which the rest of the application will be calculated accordingly.

Once the user enters, they will see the top trending headlines that are filtered loosely to what they are comfortable seeing.

For example, if the user ranked themselves as an extreme liberal, they will see articles from outlets with a liberal bias.

HOW A NEWS OUTLET'S POSITION IS DETERMINED

Most major news outlets have a clear media bias, so we ranked those on the same scale of 1-10. If a particular outlet didn’t have an obvious position, we ranked it slightly more ambiguously but to our best judgement after thorough research, like a 4-6 out of 10.


PERSPECTIVE FUNCTION

Here’s the gold.


Instead of scratching the surface and ranking each article from left to right, we wanted to delve into creating an app that truly worked to each user’s specific perspective.

So, we wrote an algorithm that shows the user articles based on how similar or different it is to their selected political position.






EQUATION

The equation is simple.


Each article is assigned a number equal to the number that its source outlet is ranked.

We take that number, and subtract it from the user’s assigned number, and then take the absolute value of the result.

The result is then translated back to the user interface as a red bar to indicate how different the article’s perspective is to the user’s perspective.



FUTURE DEVELOPMENT

SMALL

We’d like to add a simple like or dislike function on each article and store it into our database to mold your perspective as it grows from using Untrending.

MEDIUM

We’re calculating our algorithm based on a linear spectrum at the moment, but we would like to deepen our accuracy by expanding our system to a four quadrant graph as most political spectrums are.

LARGE

As the United States continues to become more and more diverse as a nation, it’s difficult for those who immigrate or come from a different ethnic background to engage in US politics, yet it affects them equally if not more than it does the American rooted. Perhaps starting by translating and transcribing articles from English news stories into different languages, it would be revolutionary to allow these disconnected communities a window to see into our world as we see it.
